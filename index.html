Introduction

How can autonomous robots and vehicles achieve high-precision prediction with social intelligence in complex human-robot interaction scenarios? Behavior prediction is essential in crowded environments like factories, hospitals, and shopping malls, as well as in traffic scenes where intelligent vehicles and pedestrians coexist. Nevertheless, it faces critical challenges: highly complex interactions, multi-modal dependencies, uncertain pedestrian intentions, and dynamically changing contexts. This workshop will explore prediction problems in these real-world settings. This workshop aims to enhance system safety, interaction fluency, and social acceptance. By integrating multidisciplinary approaches from machine learning, artificial intelligence, kinematics, and cognitive psychology, it will transcend conventional technical frameworks to collectively explore next-generation prediction paradigms suited for dynamic social environments.

Topics

This workshop covers fundamental and applied research on pedestrian behavior prediction for autonomous systems. Key topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Modeling pedestrian interactions with robots and autonomous vehicles (AVs).
  • Leveraging multi-modal sensing to enhance behavior prediction.
  • Understanding how environmental context and social norms influence interactions.
  • Modeling pedestrian behavior in both 2D and 3D spaces.
  • Capturing the inherent stochasticity in pedestrian actions during social interactions.
  • Quantifying and reasoning about uncertainty in predictions.
  • Integrating psychological insights to improve behavior models.
  • Developing real-time prediction systems for dynamic environments.
  • Developing, curating, and evaluating datasets for pedestrian behavior prediction.
  • Applying algorithmic approaches, including deep learning, probabilistic models, and hybrid frameworks.
  • Combining insights from psychology, cognitive science, neuroscience, and robotics for holistic modeling.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions of original research papers, case studies, and reviews related to pedestrian behavior prediction. Submissions should address one or more of the key topics outlined above. All papers will be peer-reviewed, and accepted papers will be presented at the workshop.
